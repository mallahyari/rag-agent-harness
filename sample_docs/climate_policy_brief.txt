Climate Policy Brief — Carbon Markets Update (2025)

Overview
--------
Global carbon markets reached a record trading volume of $950 billion in 2024,
a 34% increase over 2023. The European Union Emissions Trading System (EU ETS)
remains the world's largest carbon market, accounting for 45% of global volume.

EU ETS Developments
--------------------
The EU ETS price averaged €68 per tonne of CO2 in 2024, up from €52 in 2023.
The Market Stability Reserve (MSR) continued to withdraw allowances, tightening supply.
Phase 4 (2021–2030) includes annual linear reduction factor of 2.2%.

US Voluntary Carbon Market
--------------------------
The US voluntary carbon market grew to $210 billion in 2024.
Nature-based solutions (reforestation, soil carbon) represent 38% of US voluntary credits.
The Integrity Council for the Voluntary Carbon Market (ICVCM) issued new Core Carbon
Principles in late 2024, raising quality standards for credit issuance.

Emerging Markets
----------------
China's national ETS covered 8.5 billion tonnes of CO2 in 2024, making it the
world's largest by volume, though price levels remain low at around ¥80/tonne (~$11).
India launched its Carbon Credit Trading Scheme (CCTS) in early 2025, targeting
energy-intensive industries including steel, cement, and aluminum.

Key Policy Trends
-----------------
1. Carbon border adjustment mechanisms (CBAM) are being adopted beyond the EU.
2. Article 6 of the Paris Agreement implementation is progressing, enabling
   international transfers of carbon credits between countries.
3. Corporate Science-Based Targets (SBTs) are driving increased demand for
   high-quality carbon credits with strong co-benefits.

Challenges
----------
- Additionality concerns remain for some offset project types.
- Double-counting risks in Article 6 implementation require careful oversight.
- Greenwashing allegations have led to increased scrutiny of corporate carbon claims.
